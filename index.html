Short-answer practice

Explain (in 2–3 sentences): why Rutherford’s gold foil experiment changed the “plum pudding” model.

Rutherford observed rare, large-angle deflections of alpha particles, implying a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus. Most particles passed through, showing atoms are mostly empty space, which contradicted Thomson’s diffuse positive-charge model.
